Goyo's Achievements and Contributions

After he had achieved his bachelor's degree at Ateneo de Manila,
he presented himself at military service of when revolution broke out in 1896.
There, his skills at war and daring attacks in various encounters were highly noticed by
Emilio Aguinaldo; Therefore, making him steadily promoted in the military as a Brigadier General
and Aguinaldo's protege and confidential man. He had encountered many wars/battles during his time
such as the Battle of Kakarong de Sili, Raid at Paombong, Battle of Pasong Balite, Battle of Quingua,
Battle of Calumpit, and Battle of Tirad Pass.

He was also loyal to the Alligiances that he is a part of (e.g. First Philippine Republic,
Republic of Biak-na-bato, Katipunan, and Kakarong Republic) , He was known for his last stand at Tirad Pass,
his victory on the first phase Battle of Quingua, and of course,the successful assault on Spanish barracks. After he had
been designated by Aguinaldo as Dictator of the provinces of Bulacan and Nueva Ecija, On June 1, 1898,
he landed at Kay Luntaw with rifles, established his headquarters at Matungao and started the seige of Bulacan.
